Landing gear, flaps and economy

The crew of the ill-fated Il-86 also used a new technique and released the flaps to the landing gear. Unaware of the new trends in piloting, the Il-86 automatics immediately turned on the voice and light alarms, which required the crew to release the landing gear. So that the alarm system did not annoy the pilots, it was simply turned off, as one turns off a sleepy alarm clock. Now there was no one to remind the crew that the landing gear still needed to be released. Today, however, there are already copies of Tu-154 and Il-86 aircraft with modified alarms, which fly according to the approach method with a late release of mechanization.

Course and glide system
The course and glide path system consists of two parts: pairs of directional and glide path beacons. Two directional beacons are located behind the runway and emit a directional radio signal along it at different frequencies at small angles. On the runway centerline, the intensity of both signals is the same. To the left and right of this line, the signal from one of the beacons is stronger than the other. By comparing the intensity of the signals, the aircraft's radio navigation system determines which side and how far it is from the centerline. Two glide path beacons are located in the area of the landing zone and operate in the same way, only in the vertical plane.
